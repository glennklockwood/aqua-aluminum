I am a performance analyst at NERSC, the National Energy Research Scientific Computing Center at Lawrence Berkeley National Laboratory. I am a materials scientist by training, and I used to study the chemical physics of water/silica interfaces with molecular dynamics simulations. At some point I made the decision to pursue my interest in supercomputing as a career, and I now work as a specialist in data-intensive and high-performance computing technologies. More information about me is available on my CV and on LinkedIn.

This site contains various notes I've taken related to technical computing over the years. I welcome any feedback about its contents at the e-mail address listed at the bottom of every page, and I can also be found in #hpc on freenode.
